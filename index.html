Properties of Determinant

Key Properties

  • Maximum number of terms in the determinant of an n × n matrix is n!
  • For a lower or upper triangular matrix, the determinant is the product of diagonal elements.
  • If two rows or two columns are identical, then the determinant is 0.
  • If two rows or two columns are interchanged, the sign of the determinant changes.
  • det(A) = det(AT) = det(1/A-1)
  • If matrix A is multiplied by a scalar k, then det(kA) = kn × det(A), where A is n × n.
Important Note:
  • Simultaneous row and column operations should not be used while computing determinant.
